Summary

Perhaps the most famous cryptid in the world — Bigfoot, also known as Sasquatch, is a purported giant ape like creature primarily known through numerous claimed sightings in wilderness and forested areas of Pacific Northwest America.

Scientists discount the existence of Bigfoot due to the lack of physical evidence and the size of the breeding groups that would be required for it to continue as a viable creature.

However the large number of sightings from credible people, and the famous 16mm file taken by Roger Patterson have convinced many that Bigfoot like creatures do really exist in the United states.

Is Bigfoot real?

Evidence for

Sightings

There have been Sasquatch sightings for centuries in North America, with thousands of people claiming to have seen Bigfoot like creatures, usually in sparsely populated areas of the country. Mainstream science dismisses all of these as either misidentifications, for example brown bears, or as hoaxes.

Eyewitnesses evidence is notoriously unreliable and fakery and hoax have plagued serious study of Bigfoot for years, but nethertheless there remains a significant number of eyewitness accounts that cannot easily be dismissed. Reliable witnesses such as policeman, forest rangers and medical professionals have all given detailed and convincing eyewitness statements.

One US Forest Service Office, an experienced outdoorsman, observed a large, jet black creature on a beach in Oregon in 1995, and later took several photographs of the oversized footprints it left in the sand. Other encounters with trained professionals are equally well documented. Many authoritative observers fear coming forward in case they risk their jobs and reputations.

Patterson-Gimlin film

A 16mm film taken by Roger Patterson and Roger Gimlin in 1967 remains the best known and most credible of all Bigfoot films. Allegedly showing a female Bigfoot crossing a clearing in Bluff Creek California, the Patterson Gimlin film has been subject to fierce debate ever since over its veracity. Any attempt to definitely debunk or authenticate the film have proven fruitless, and the filmmakers have consistently rejected any claim that they hoaxed the footage by filming a man in a suit.

Footprints

Numerous casts and photographs have appeared over the years purporting to show Bigfoot footprints. Renowned primatologist John Napier was one of the first experts to treat the prints seriously. He studied hundreds of casts and photographs and interviewed witnesses and amater investigators. Ultimately whilst Napier’s research was inconclusive he didn't dismiss the idea that Bigfoot could be a real creature entirely. However with no real Bigfoot tracks to compare against, the prints are possible to authenticate and with fraud widespread, they remain inconclusive either way.

Evidence against

Lack of physical evidence

Whilst man has hunted evidence of Bigfoot for decades, in all of that time not a single verifiable Bigfoot skeleton, bone or carcass has ever been found. Numerous examples of alleged Bigfoot hair and blood have been found, but they invariable turn out to be either hair from other known animals or of inconclusive origin.***

Number of hoaxes

If one thing has damaged Bigfoot research as a serious discipline its the sheer number hoaxes that have plagued the field. Fakery and fraud is rife, often cheerfully admitted by the perpetrators. And its not a new phenomenon — Rant Mullens revealed in 1982 that he and his friends had carved giant Bigfoot tracks and used them to fake footprints as far back as 1930. The most notorious modern fraudsters are Ray Wallace, who claimed to have faked 100s of Bigfoot tracks and Rick Dyer, who announced to the world in 2008 that he had captured a Bigfoot corpse only for it to turn out to be a Halloween costume stuffed with roadkill and entrails.

Lack of quality film evidence

Considering the large number of sightings and the modern ubiquity of camera phones, there remain few credible films or photographs that have clearly captured a creature that may be Bigfoot. In fact, aside from the Patterson film, most films supposedly showing Bigfoot are extremely poor quality, blurry and entirely inconclusive.
